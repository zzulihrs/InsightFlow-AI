# OpenAI Raises $122 Billion to Accelerate the Next Phase of AI

**Source:** OpenAI Blog
**Date:** March 31, 2026
**Original URL:** https://openai.com/index/accelerating-the-next-phase-ai/

---

OpenAI has announced the close of a landmark funding round, securing $122 billion in committed capital at a post-money valuation of $852 billion. The investment marks one of the largest capital raises in the technology sector and is aimed at accelerating the next phase of artificial intelligence development, positioning the company as a core infrastructure provider for the global AI ecosystem.

## Growth and Momentum

The company emphasized that its rapid growth is being driven by a powerful flywheel of consumer adoption, enterprise deployment, developer activity, and access to large-scale compute resources. With platforms like ChatGPT serving as a gateway into workplaces and daily life, demand is shifting beyond basic AI tools toward intelligent systems that can transform business operations. This momentum is reinforced by OpenAI's APIs and developer tools such as Codex, which enable users to build and scale applications efficiently.

OpenAI highlighted its unprecedented growth trajectory, noting that it became the fastest technology platform to reach 10 million and 100 million users, and is now approaching 1 billion weekly active users. Financially, the company reported rapid revenue expansion -- from reaching $1 billion annually within a year of launching ChatGPT to generating $2 billion in monthly revenue. This pace, it says, surpasses that of earlier internet giants like Alphabet and Meta at comparable stages. Added intelligence makes AI useful for more complex workflows, which increases usage, drives compute demand, and accelerates the next turn of the flywheel, creating a compounding effect where better infrastructure and better models lower the cost of delivery, while improved products and deeper enterprise deployment increase revenue per unit of compute.

## Investor Consortium

The funding round attracted strong backing from global investors, led by strategic partners including Amazon, NVIDIA, and SoftBank Group, with continued participation from Microsoft. Additional institutional investors such as BlackRock, Sequoia Capital, Temasek, Andreessen Horowitz, D.E. Shaw Ventures, MGX, TPG, and ARK Invest also joined the round, reflecting broad confidence in OpenAI's long-term vision.

The company also expanded participation to individual investors through banking channels for the first time, raising over $3 billion from retail investors. In addition, OpenAI expanded its revolving credit facility to approximately $4.7 billion, supported by a global syndicate of financial institutions including JPMorgan Chase, Citigroup, Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, Wells Fargo, Mizuho, Royal Bank of Canada, SMBC, UBS, HSBC, and Santander.

## Product and Platform Advances

On the product front, OpenAI continues to roll out advancements across its ecosystem. The company's latest model, GPT-5.4, described as the most capable model to date, already powers 900 million weekly users and more than 50 million paid subscribers. Product improvements span personalization, multimodal capabilities, coding, search, and enterprise solutions.

ChatGPT remains a dominant force in consumer AI, with hundreds of millions of weekly active users and strong engagement metrics. Enterprise adoption now accounts for more than 40 percent of revenue and is on track to reach parity with consumer usage by the end of 2026. OpenAI's APIs process more than 15 billion tokens per minute, while its coding product Codex has surpassed 2 million weekly users with 70 percent month-over-month growth. Internal advertising pilots generated $100 million in annualized recurring revenue within six weeks of launch.

## Compute and Infrastructure Strategy

A central pillar of OpenAI's strategy is its investment in compute infrastructure, which underpins both research and product delivery. The company described durable access to compute as the strategic advantage that compounds across the entire system: it advances research, improves products, expands access, and structurally lowers the cost of delivery at scale.

OpenAI is expanding beyond a limited set of providers to a diversified infrastructure model spanning multiple cloud platforms, chipmakers, and data center partners. While NVIDIA GPUs remain foundational, the company is also collaborating with partners such as Oracle, Amazon Web Services, Google Cloud, and CoreWeave for infrastructure, and working across different chip platforms including systems from AMD, Cerebras, and custom silicon developed in partnership with Broadcom. Approximately $18 billion of the funding is reportedly earmarked for the Stargate project, a $500 billion initiative with SoftBank and Oracle to build next-generation AI data centers.

## Unified AI Superapp Vision

Looking ahead, OpenAI is focused on building a unified AI "superapp" that integrates ChatGPT, Codex, its Atlas browser, and agent-based capabilities into a single, seamless user experience. This approach is designed to simplify access, accelerate adoption, and enable users to move from intent to action across multiple workflows within one platform. The company discontinued Sora, its video generation product, to focus resources on this unified platform strategy.

## A Defining Moment

The company framed this funding milestone as part of a broader historical moment, comparing the current wave of AI investment to the infrastructure buildouts that powered earlier economic transformations. As Sam Altman stated: "In past generations, capital markets helped build the systems that defined modern economies, from electricity to highways to the internet. This is that kind of moment again. The capital being deployed today is helping build the infrastructure layer for intelligence itself."

With this new capital, OpenAI aims to expand access to intelligent systems globally, drive economic productivity, and shape the future of AI as a foundational layer of modern society. The company, while generating extraordinary revenue growth, remains unprofitable and is expected to pursue an IPO by late 2026. CFO Sarah Friar indicated the company will be included in ARK Invest ETFs as part of its path toward becoming a publicly traded entity.
