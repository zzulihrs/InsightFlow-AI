# Retiring GPT-4o, GPT-4.1, GPT-4.1 mini, and OpenAI o4-mini in ChatGPT

**Source:** OpenAI Blog
**Date:** January 29, 2026
**Original URL:** https://openai.com/index/retiring-gpt-4o-and-older-models/

---

On February 13, 2026, alongside the previously announced retirement of GPT-5 (Instant and Thinking), we will retire GPT-4o, GPT-4.1, GPT-4.1 mini, and OpenAI o4-mini from ChatGPT.

In the API, there are no changes at this time.

We are announcing the upcoming retirement of GPT-4o because improvements are now in place, and because the vast majority of usage has shifted to GPT-5.2, with only 0.1% of users still choosing GPT-4o each day.

After we first deprecated it and later restored access during the GPT-5 release, we learned more about how people actually use it day to day. We brought GPT-4o back after hearing clear feedback from a subset of Plus and Pro users, who told us they needed more time to transition key use cases, like creative ideation, and that they preferred GPT-4o's conversational style and warmth.

That feedback directly shaped GPT-5.1 and GPT-5.2, with improvements to personality, stronger support for creative ideation, and more ways to customize how ChatGPT responds. You can choose from base styles and tones like Friendly, and controls for things like warmth and enthusiasm.

GPTs using retired models will automatically move to the closest GPT-5.3 Instant and GPT-5.4 Thinking and Pro equivalent.

We know that losing access to GPT-4o will feel frustrating for some users, and we didn't make this decision lightly. Retiring models is never easy, but it allows us to focus on improving the models most people use today.

## Background and Context

GPT-4o was originally introduced in May 2024 as OpenAI's first unified multimodal architecture capable of processing text, audio, and images natively. It quickly became the default ChatGPT model and was widely praised for its conversational warmth and creative capabilities.

When GPT-5 launched in August 2025, OpenAI initially retired all previous models. This decision was met with significant user backlash. Within days, CEO Sam Altman reversed the decision and reinstated GPT-4o due to overwhelming community feedback. Users expressed that they needed more time to transition and that GPT-4o had unique qualities, particularly in creative ideation and conversational style, that they valued.

OpenAI then committed to a more gradual transition approach. In November 2025, the company announced that GPT-5 Instant and Thinking models would be deprecated within three months. The January 29, 2026 announcement extended this retirement to GPT-4o and the remaining older models.

The user response to the final retirement announcement was notable. A Change.org petition gathered over 22,000 signatures to "save" GPT-4o, and a #keep4o movement emerged on social media and Reddit, with users describing emotional attachment to the model.

OpenAI emphasized that GPT-5.2, released in December 2025, offers more customization options for users who valued GPT-4o's distinctive style. The newer model includes configurable personality traits, base styles and tones, and controls for warmth and enthusiasm, features that were developed in direct response to user feedback about what made GPT-4o special.

For API users, the retirement applies only to ChatGPT at this time. The retired models continue to be available through the API, with no immediate changes announced for API access.
